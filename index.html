Real-time AQI in Malaysia

AQI, also known as Air Quality Index, is a numerical index that represents the quality of the air based on various pollutants such as particulate matter, carbon monoxide, sulfur dioxide, nitrogen dioxide, and ozone. Real-time AQI in Malaysia provides up-to-date information about the current air quality levels in different regions of the country.

Introduction of Air Pollution

Air pollution refers to the presence of harmful substances in the Earth's atmosphere that can negatively impact human health, the environment, and the overall quality of life. It is caused by the release of harmful pollutants into the air from various sources, both natural and human-made.

Factors of Air Pollution

Air pollution is influenced by a variety of factors, both natural and human-related. These factors contribute to the release and accumulation of pollutants in the atmosphere, leading to degraded air quality. Understanding these factors is crucial for developing effective strategies to mitigate and control air pollution.

Effects of Air Pollution

Air pollution has wide-ranging and significant effects on human health, the environment, and the economy. These effects can be both immediate and long-term, impacting various aspects of life. By examining these effects, we can gain a deeper understanding to address this pressing issue and work towards cleaner air for all.
