Factors of Air
Pollution

Traffic

Despite the fast growing interest in electric cars, most of the vehicles are powered by gasoline and diesel engines that burn petroleum to release energy. Burning petroleum with enough oxygen should produce nothing worse than carbon dioxide and water. However, in practice, fuels aren't pure hydrocarbons and engines don't burn them cleanly. As a result, exhausts from engine contain all kinds of pollution, such as notably particulates, carbon monoxide, nitrogen oxides, volatile organic compounds, and lead.

Power Plants

Renewable energy sources are helping us generate a bigger proportion of our power every year, but the overwhelming majority of electricity is still produced by burning fossil fuels such as coal, gas, and oil, mostly in conventional power plants. Just like car engines, power plants should produce nothing worse than carbon dioxide and water. However, in practice, fuels are dirty and they don't burn cleanly, so power plants produce a range of air pollutants, such as notably sulfur dioxide, nitrogen oxides, and particulates.

Factories

Factories often release small but significant quantities of pollution into the air. For example, factories that produce metals such as aluminum and steel, refine petroleum, produce cement, synthesize plastic, or make other chemicals are among those that can produce harmful air pollution. Most factories that pollute release small amounts of pollution continually over a long period of time. However, sometimes factories can release huge amounts of air pollution accidentally in a very short space of time.

Others

Although traffic, power plants, and factories produce the majority of Earth's outdoor air pollution, many other factors contribute to the problem too. The causes of indoor air pollution are very different. Burning solid fuels for cooking and heating produces indoor air pollution that can seriously harm our health. In some areas, garbage is incinerated instead of being recycled or landfilled and that can also produce significant air pollution unless the incinerators are properly designed.
